Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic health condition characterized by elevated levels of blood glucose, commonly referred to as blood sugar. This occurs when the body either doesn't produce enough insulin or can't effectively use the insulin it produces. Insulin is a hormone that regulates blood sugar and allows it to enter cells to be used for energy.

What are the symptoms of diabetes?

Thirst, frequent urination, and unexplained weight loss.

What are the risk factors for diabetes?

Obesity, family history, and lack of physical activity.

How can diabetes be managed or prevented?

Medication, lifestyle changes, and regular monitoring of blood sugar levels.
