Heart Disease

Heart disease refers to a range of conditions that affect the heart, including coronary artery disease, heart rhythm problems (arrhythmias), and heart defects present at birth (congenital heart defects). It is a leading cause of death worldwide, with various risk factors contributing to its development.

What are the symptoms of heart disease?

Chest pain, shortness of breath, and irregular heartbeat.

What are the risk factors for heart disease?

High blood pressure, high cholesterol, and smoking.

How can heart disease be managed or prevented?

Healthy diet, regular exercise, and avoiding tobacco smoke.
